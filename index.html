Bryan K. Lynn

I am a mathematical and computational biologist, a dabbler in microbiology, and a science educator. I use this interdisciplinary approach to answer questions about how behavior and interactions influence populations. My PhD dissertation examines how cooperation, or the lack thereof, influences population dynamics.

About

My research uses a combination of mathematical theory, computational analyses, and lab experiments to study how behavior and species interactions influence population dynamics. Broadly, I am interested in population modeling and examining model and parameter choices in biological research. See my past and current research projects here.

I also have interests in examining biological theories through the lens of social science and philosophy theories to ask questions like: "if evolution is driven by maximizing fitness and offspring production, why is same sex-mating so prevalant"?
